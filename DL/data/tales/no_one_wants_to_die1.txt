An old woodcutter, too weary to pick up his load of sticks,
exclaimed: "I wish that Death would take me!" Even as he spoke, Death
appeared, but seeing him, the old man changed his mind. Now his only
request was: "Would you help me lift this load to my
shoulders?" 
 Source: Retold from " The Old Man and Death ," Jacobs,  The
Fables of Aesop,  no. 69, type 845.
